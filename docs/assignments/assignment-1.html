My first assignment has two parts.

(a) Conversations on Data Science and Industrial Engineering - Baykal Hafızoğlu & Erdi Daşdemir

The video I chose for this assignment is “Conversations on Data Science and Industrial Engineering” featuring Baykal Hafızoğlu and Erdi Daşdemir.

Baykal Hafızoğlu completed his bachelor’s and master’s degrees at METU in 2005 and 2007, respectively, and subsequently earned his Ph.D. from Arizona State University. The companies he has worked for include LLamasoft, Predit, and FICO, and his roles have spanned operations research, optimization, and supply chain analytics.

Mr. Baykal currently serves as Director of Platform Management at FICO in the U.S.

Hafızoğlu discusses four categories within the analytical field:

-Descriptive,

-Diagnostic

-Predictive

-Prescriptive analytics.

He notes that an industrial engineering education already covers these four areas and therefore emphasizes the value of the curriculum’s content. He points out that many data science master’s programs actually cover material that industrial engineering students have already learned.

One of his strongest arguments relates to implementation: He argues that an analytical model has no real value if it is not delivered to end users. In one of his projects at Procter & Gamble, he developed a “Product Life Cycle Management” application containing 50,000 lines of code; however, he notes that only 100 of those lines actually comprised the optimization model. The remaining code handled data processing, the user interface, and software engineering tasks. This demonstrates that making a model usable is just as important as creating it.

  1. Open-ended Question:

    Why is it important to establish a clear problem definition with measurable KPIs when starting a project? What kinds of issues might arise without this?

    Answer:

    Without a clear and measurable problem definition, project teams risk losing their direction and failing to communicate effectively with stakeholders. All parties involved in the process must be able to express their communication using measurable metrics (KPI’s) rather than vague statements. Without this foundation, even the most advanced analytical model becomes meaningless because success cannot be measured.

    Additionally, defining success criteria in advance enables teams to objectively evaluate their solutions and demonstrate the solution’s value to decision-makers. Furthermore, tracking conflicting KPI’s is equally important, as improving one metric may inadvertently worsen another.
